# Why AIR Control?

Understanding the risk and obligations of an AI product early is crucial. The EU AI Act introduces strict regulatory boundaries with severe consequences for non-compliance. AIR Control helps you navigate this complexity.

## The Importance of Early Evaluation
- **Communicate Effectively**: Generate clear, audience-specific reports (Engineering, Legal, Executive) so the entire organization understands the product's regulatory impact.
- **Uncover Hidden Traps**: Identify features that might trigger a "Risk Transformation" — minor technical details that can unexpectedly elevate a product into a High or Unacceptable risk category.
- **Assess Penalties**: Understand the severe financial penalties under the Act (up to 7% of global turnover) before committing to development.
- **Guide Go/No-Go Decisions**: Evaluate if the cost of managing the compliance overhead is commercially viable before building the product.

# AIR Control Guide

## How to Use It
1. Enter a detailed product description in the text box.
2. Click **Classify Product** to run the analysis pipeline.
3. Review the outputs and generated reports. 

## Providing Feedback ("I Disagree")
If you disagree with the classification or mapping provided by the AI, you can click the **Thumbs Down / I Disagree** button in the top navigation. This opens a dialog where you can:
- **Correct the Classification**: Specify what you believe the correct Risk Level or Role should be.
- **Provide a Rationale**: Explain your reasoning so the engine's logic can be reviewed.
- **Map Evidence**: Link specific snippets from your description to support your correction.
This feedback loop is crucial for auditing the reasoning and improving the engine over time.

## Pipeline Stages
- **Signals**: Evaluates the description against a Signal Quality Matrix to ensure there is enough clarity to proceed.
- **Classification**: Determines the Primary Risk category (e.g., High, Limited) and Role (e.g., Provider, Deployer).
- **Map**: Links specific snippets from your description to exact articles and annexes in the Act.
- **Warnings**: Highlights potential scenarios where the risk classification could escalate (Risk Transformation).
- **Info**: Outlines the standard informational obligations your system must comply with.

## Reports & Communication
The final stage compiles targeted reports for different stakeholders:
- **Engineering**: Outlines technical constraints, data logging needs, and architectural boundaries.
- **Legal**: Highlights jurisdictional relevancy, compliance readiness, and regulatory impact.
- **Executive**: Provides a definitive Go/No-Go strategic decision with an evidence-based rationale, helping Product Managers clearly communicate top-line financial exposure and market risk.
